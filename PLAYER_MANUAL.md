## Welcome to Bathala

### What is Bathala?

**Bathala** is a card-based adventure game inspired by Filipino mythology. You play as a **babaylan** (a spiritual seer) exploring a mystical realm to restore cosmic order and find the lost creator god, Bathala.

### What Makes It Special?

- **üÉè Poker-Style Combat**: Form poker hands (like pairs, straights, and flushes) to power your actions
- **üé® Strategic Deck Building**: Start with 52 cards and sculpt your perfect deck by removing weak cards and upgrading strong ones
- **üåì Day & Night Cycles**: Every action advances time‚Äîenemies are calm by day but aggressive at night
- **‚è±Ô∏è Race Against Time**: You have 5 cycles (about 500 moves) to prepare before facing each chapter's boss
- **üé≠ Your Choices Matter**: Spare or slay defeated enemies to shape your path and rewards
- **üìä Smart Difficulty**: The game adapts to your skill level to keep you challenged but not frustrated
- **üè∫ Rich Filipino Culture**: Meet creatures from Philippine folklore like the Tikbalang, Bakunawa, and Kapre

## How to Play

### The Core Loop

Every adventure follows this pattern:

1. **Explore the Map**: Move across a grid-based world
2. **Enter Encounters**: Fight enemies, visit shops, rest at campsites
3. **Make Choices**: Spare or slay defeated enemies
4. **Improve Your Deck**: Remove weak cards, upgrade good ones
5. **Face the Boss**: After 5 complete day-night cycles
6. **Repeat**: Move to the next chapter or start a new run

### The Five-Cycle Countdown

Time in Bathala is measured in **actions**, not seconds:

- **1 Cycle = 100 Actions**
  - 50 actions during Day
  - 50 actions during Night
- **5 Cycles = 500 Actions** before the boss appears

**What counts as an action?**
- Moving on the map
- Entering battles
- Visiting shops or rest sites
- Any choice you make

**Strategy Tip**: Plan your route carefully! You want to be strong enough to face the boss by cycle 5.

### Day vs. Night

#### ‚òÄÔ∏è **Day Phase**
- Enemies won't attack unless you approach them
- Better visibility on the map
- Standard shop prices
- Perfect time to explore and prepare

#### üåô **Night Phase**
- Enemies actively hunt you down
- Reduced map visibility
- More dangerous encounters
- Better rewards for taking risks

## Combat Guide

### How Combat Works

```
1. Draw 8 cards from your deck
2. Choose 5 cards to form a poker hand
3. Pick an action: Attack, Defend, or Special
4. Your action happens (powered by your hand)
5. Enemy takes their turn
6. Repeat until you win or lose
```

### The Four Elements

Your deck contains four suits (elements), each with unique powers:

#### üî• **Apoy (Fire)**
- **Effect**: Burns enemies for damage over time
- **Style**: Aggressive, fast damage
- **Best For**: Ending fights quickly

#### üíß **Tubig (Water)**
- **Effect**: Heals your health
- **Style**: Defensive, outlasting enemies
- **Best For**: Surviving tough battles

#### üåç **Lupa (Earth)**
- **Effect**: Increases your attack power (Strength)
- **Style**: Balanced, building momentum
- **Best For**: Consistent damage strategies

#### üí® **Hangin (Air)**
- **Effect**: Increases your defense (Dexterity)
- **Style**: Tactical, avoiding damage
- **Best For**: Protecting yourself while attacking

### Status Effects Simplified

**Good for You**:
- **Strength**: Your attacks do more damage
- **Dexterity**: You block more damage
- **Block**: Absorbs damage before it hurts you

**Bad for You**:
- **Burn**: Lose health each turn
- **Weak**: Your attacks do less damage (-25%)
- **Vulnerable**: You take more damage (+50%)
- **Stun**: Skip your next turn
- **Seal**: Can't use Special actions
- **Fear**: Randomly discard cards

### The Discard Button

**What it does**: Lets you throw away up to 5 cards and draw new ones

**Uses**: You start with **3 discards per combat** (some items give you more)

**When to use it**:
- Your 8 cards are all terrible
- You need a specific element
- Hunting for a better poker hand
- Emergency situations
- You have 2-3 charges left to experiment

**When to save it**:
- Your cards are already good
- Fighting a boss (save for critical moments)
- You have only 1 discard left

## Building Your Deck

Unlike most card games where you keep adding cards, Bathala is about **refinement**. You start with 52 cards and make them better.

### The Three Ways to Improve

#### üíÄ **Purify (Remove Cards)**
**Where**: Shops  
**Cost**: 50-100 gold per card  
**What it does**: Permanently removes a card from your deck

**Why remove cards?**
- Smaller deck = more consistent hands
- Get rid of weak low-value cards (1, 2, 3)
- Focus on your favorite elements
- Make room for better cards

**Beginner Strategy**:
1. Remove all "1" value cards first (4 cards)
2. Remove all "2" and "3" value cards (8 more cards)
3. You've removed 12 cards‚Äîalready much better!

#### ‚ú® **Attune (Upgrade Cards)**
**Where**: Rest Sites (campsites)  
**Cost**: Free!  
**What it does**: Transforms a card into a higher-value card of the **same element**

**Example**: Fire-5 ‚Üí Fire-9 ‚Üí Fire-13

**Strategy**:
- Focus on one or two elements
- Turn weak cards into face cards (11, 12, 13)
- The higher the value, the better your hands

#### ‚ûï **Infuse (Add Cards)**
**Where**: Elite and Boss rewards (rare!)  
**Cost**: Free (it's a reward)  
**What it does**: Adds a powerful new card

**Warning**: Don't add too many cards! Quality over quantity.

### Deck Building Strategies

#### **Beginner-Friendly: Balanced Deck**
- Keep all four elements
- Remove only the lowest cards (1-3)
- Final size: 40-44 cards
- **Pro**: Works in any situation
- **Con**: Less powerful than focused decks

#### **Intermediate: Dual-Element**
- Choose two elements (e.g., Fire + Earth)
- Remove all cards from the other two
- Upgrade your chosen elements
- Final size: 30-36 cards
- **Pro**: Strong and flexible
- **Con**: Needs planning

#### **Advanced: Mono-Element**
- Keep only one element
- Remove everything else
- Maximum upgrade focus
- Final size: 24-30 cards
- **Pro**: Most powerful element effects
- **Con**: Vulnerable to counters, expensive

#### **Expert: Face Card Focus**
- Keep mostly 10, 11, 12, 13 value cards
- Upgrade lower cards to faces
- Remove all low values
- Final size: 20-26 cards
- **Pro**: Consistently strong hands
- **Con**: Very expensive, hard early game

### Deck Size Guide

| Size | Consistency | Power | Difficulty |
|------|-------------|-------|------------|
| 20-26 cards | Excellent | High | Expert |
| 28-34 cards | Very Good | Medium-High | Intermediate |
| 36-42 cards | Good | Medium | Beginner |
| 44-52 cards | Fair | Low-Medium | Starter |

## Exploring the World

### Map Types

You'll encounter different locations as you explore:

#### ‚öîÔ∏è **Combat Encounters** (Common)
- Fight regular enemies
- Earn gold
- Option to remove one card for free after victory
- **Risk**: Low

#### üíÄ **Elite Encounters** (Uncommon)
- Fight tougher enemies
- Earn relics or rare card additions
- More gold than normal
- **Risk**: High

#### üî• **Rest Sites** (Uncommon)
Choose one:
- **Rest**: Heal 30% of your max health
- **Attune**: Upgrade a card for free

#### üè™ **Shops** (Uncommon)
- Buy relics (100-200 gold)
- Buy potions (40-80 gold)
- Purify cards (50-100 gold)
- Sell items you don't want (50% value back)

#### ‚ùì **Mysterious Events** (Rare)
- Story encounters
- Sometimes get free items
- Unlock lore entries
- Usually low risk

#### üëë **Boss** (Once per chapter)
- Appears after 5 cycles
- Very tough fight
- Chapter completion
- **Risk**: Very High

### Navigation Tips

**Early Game (Cycles 1-2)**:
- Visit shops to start purifying cards
- Take some combats to earn gold
- Rest when below 60% health

**Mid Game (Cycles 3-4)**:
- Hunt for elite encounters (if ready)
- Continue deck refinement
- Collect powerful relics

**Late Game (Cycle 5)**:
- Rest before the boss
- Buy potions for the fight
- Avoid risky encounters

## Advanced Tips

### Combat Strategy

**Opening Turns**:
- High health? Attack aggressively
- Medium health? Balance attack and defense
- Low health? Defend first, stabilize

**Mid-Fight**:
- Enemy below 30% HP? Finish them off
- Enemy charging special attack? Defend next turn
- You're below 50% HP? Consider using a potion

**Using Your Discard**:
- All low-value cards? Discard immediately
- Looking for specific element? Discard off-element cards
- Good hand already? Save your discard

### Deck Optimization

**The 30-Card Rule**:
- **Why**: 30 cards = 26.7% chance to draw any specific card
- **How**: Remove 22 cards through purification
- **Result**: Much more consistent hands

**Upgrade Priority**:
1. Your most-used element
2. Cards you always keep in hands
3. High-value cards (make them 10-13)
4. Support elements second

**When to Stop Purifying**:
- You're consistently getting good hands
- You have 28-32 cards left
- You've spent 800+ gold on removals

### Gold Management

**Early Game**:
- Save 200-300g for first shop
- Buy 1-2 relics max
- Spend rest on purifications

**Mid Game**:
- Invest in powerful relics (150-200g)
- Continue purifying if needed
- Buy 1-2 potions for boss

**Late Game**:
- Stock up on potions (3 max)
- Final deck refinements
- Save 100g for emergency

### When to Rest vs. Attune

**Choose Rest if**:
- Below 50% health
- Boss fight coming soon
- Next node is combat

**Choose Attune if**:
- Above 70% health
- Have good potions for healing
- Building specific deck strategy

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Gameplay Questions

**Q: What happens if I die?**  
A: The run ends, but you keep Spirit Fragments to buy permanent upgrades. Start a new run stronger!

**Q: Can I save during a run?**  
A: Yes! The game auto-saves after each node. Use "Continue" from main menu.

**Q: How many runs does it take to win?**  
A: Most players win within 5-10 runs after learning the systems. Permanent upgrades help!

**Q: Does the game get easier if I keep losing?**  
A: Yes! The Dynamic Difficulty system notices your performance and adjusts enemy strength.

**Q: Do my moral choices (spare/slay) affect difficulty?**  
A: No! They only affect rewards and story. Difficulty adapts to combat performance only.

### Deck Questions

**Q: What's the best deck strategy?**  
A: For beginners: Dual-Element (Fire + Earth or Water + Air). For experts: Mono-Element or Face Cards.

**Q: How many cards should I remove?**  
A: Aim for 28-34 cards total (remove 18-24 from starting 52).

**Q: Should I ever add cards?**  
A: Rarely! Only if the new card strongly synergizes with your relics.

**Q: Can I have duplicate cards?**  
A: Yes, with the "Echo of the Ancestors" relic, enabling Five of a Kind.

### Combat Questions

**Q: What's the strongest poker hand?**  
A: Straight Flush (+35 bonus), or Five of a Kind (+30, needs special relic).

**Q: Should I always form the highest hand?**  
A: Not always! Sometimes you need a specific element effect more than raw power.

**Q: How does block work?**  
A: Absorbs damage before it hurts your HP. Leftover block carries to next turn at 50% value.

**Q: What if I can't form any good hand?**  
A: Use your discard! Trade up to 5 cards for new ones.

### Item Questions

**Q: How many relics can I have?**  
A: Maximum 6 relics at once.

**Q: What if I get a 7th relic?**  
A: You choose which one to replace.

**Q: Do potions carry between runs?**  
A: No, potions are lost if unused. Spirit Fragments carry over!

**Q: What should I buy first?**  
A: Relics have the biggest impact. Buy 1-2 good relics, then focus on deck purification.

## Cultural Notes

Bathala features authentic Filipino mythology:

- **Tikbalang**: Horse-headed tricksters of the mountains
- **Bakunawa**: Giant serpent that devours the moon (causes eclipses)
- **Kapre**: Tree giants who smoke giant cigars
- **Sirena**: Benevolent mermaids (unlike Western versions)
- **Diwata**: Nature guardian spirits
- **Anito**: Ancestor and nature spirits

Playing Bathala is a journey through Philippine folklore! Each enemy has lore based on real myths.
