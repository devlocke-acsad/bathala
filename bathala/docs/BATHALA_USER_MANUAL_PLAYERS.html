1. Introduction to Bathala

Bathala is a roguelike deck-building game inspired by Filipino mythology. You play as a traveler journeying through corrupted ancestral forests, battling creatures from Philippine folklore using a unique poker-based combat system.

What is a Roguelike?

A roguelike is a type of game where:

  • Permadeath: If you lose all your health, your run ends and you start fresh
  • Procedural Generation: Each playthrough offers different encounters
  • Strategic Choices: Decisions matter - choose your path wisely
  • Progression: Get stronger throughout your run with relics and upgrades

The Story

The ancestral forests of the Philippines have been corrupted by the engkanto (enchanted beings). Creatures that once protected these sacred lands - the Tikbalang, Kapre, and Duwende - have been twisted by dark magic. You must journey through these corrupted realms, facing mythological creatures and ultimately confronting the source of the corruption.

6. Combat System

Combat in Bathala uses a unique poker-based system. You draw cards and form poker hands to attack, defend, or use special abilities.

Combat Flow

  1. Draw Phase: Draw 7 cards to your hand
  2. Selection Phase: Select 1-5 cards to play
  3. Action Phase: Choose Attack, Defend, or Special
  4. Resolution: Damage is calculated based on your hand
  5. Enemy Turn: The enemy performs their intended action
  6. Repeat: Continue until victory or defeat

Your Resources

Resource Description
Health (HP) Your life points. Reach 0 and the run ends.
Block Temporary shield that absorbs damage. Resets each turn.
Discard Charges Used to discard cards and draw new ones (3 per turn)
Ginto (Gold) Currency for buying items at the shop
üí° Tip: Watch the enemy's intent icon! It shows what they'll do next turn.

Elemental Bonuses

When using Special actions, having multiple cards of the same element gives bonus damage:

  • 1 card of same element: +1 bonus
  • 2 cards of same element: +3 bonus
  • 3 cards of same element: +6 bonus
  • 4 cards of same element: +10 bonus
  • 5 cards of same element (pure): +15 bonus
üí° Tip: Elemental bonuses only apply to Special actions, not Attack or Defend!

9. Combat Actions

After selecting your cards, choose one of three actions:

‚öîÔ∏è Attack

Deal damage to the enemy based on your hand value.

  • Best for offensive plays
  • Enhanced by Strength status effect
  • Some relics boost attack damage

üõ°Ô∏è Defend

Gain Block equal to your hand value. Block absorbs incoming damage.

  • Essential for surviving tough hits
  • Block resets at the start of your turn
  • Enhanced by Dexterity status effect

‚ú® Special

Deal damage with elemental bonuses based on the dominant suit in your hand.

  • Best when you have many cards of the same suit
  • Can trigger special relic effects
  • Great for pure element builds

Discarding Cards

You have 3 discard charges per turn. Use them wisely to:

  • Get rid of bad cards
  • Try to draw better combinations
  • Fish for specific suits or ranks
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You can only discard up to 3 times per turn. Plan ahead!

16. Tips & Strategies

Beginner Tips

  1. Watch enemy intents: The icon above enemies shows their next action
  2. Block before big hits: If an enemy shows a high damage attack, prioritize Defend
  3. Use discard charges: Don't waste them! Try to form better hands
  4. Thin your deck: Removing weak cards makes drawing good hands easier
  5. Health is a resource: Sometimes taking damage is okay to deal more

Advanced Strategies

  1. Build synergies: Collect relics that work together (e.g., attack boosters)
  2. Element focus: If going for Special attacks, stack cards of one suit
  3. Relic priority: Babaylan's Talisman is extremely powerful - upgrade your hands!
  4. Preserve HP: Your health carries between fights - don't be reckless
  5. Know the boss: Mangangaway mimics elements - have a backup plan

Hand Building Tips

  • Pairs are reliable: Easy to form, decent damage
  • Flushes for Special: 5 cards of one suit = massive elemental bonus
  • Straights are tricky: Don't force them - take what comes
  • Face cards matter: Babaylan and Datu have the highest values
